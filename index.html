Assignment: In COGS187A, Cognitive Aspects of Digital Design, we were assigned the task of evaluating the usability of University of Louisiana Lafayette's website. We have done so by using Jacob Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics. They are all listed in the menu to the left. The second part of the assignment was to present our evaluation on this website in accordance with the heuristics.

Method: We have worked in a two-person team. First we analyzed the website individually, writing down the usability errors that we encountered. Then we discussed this errors before we went through the website again together. We divided Jacob Nielsen's usability heuristics among us and wrote about our findings. We sketched the layout of this website before we begun to fill it with images and text.

Evaluation: When evaluating Louisiana.edu, we found a lot of improvement potential. The main issues are lack of consistency, invisible system status and a weak information structure on the lower levels. As a result of this the users are forced to rely on recall rather than recognition. A deeper evaluation is given under the usability heuristics listed in the menu.
