Aesthetics and minimalist design: Dialogues should not contain information which is irrelevant or rarely needed. Every extra unit of information in a dialogue competes with the relevant units of information and diminishes their relative visibility.



The Louisiana website has a lot of text and information at each page that makes the site look meesy and unclear. There are so many different menus and they sometimes change location. Some links in the different menus link to the same page, this contradicts to the rule of minimalist design. They need a new hiearchy system of the menus that users can actually understand without getting frustrated.

There is so much explanation under some of the menu options. If they feel they need so much text to explain what it is, they should probably change the name of the menu options. The menus and the page title are located at the top and to the right of the site with the details below. Although too much details at most of the pages and then occasionally a new menu is presented under the page title. The use of white space is not very good, for example the title is placed a bit to the right of the middle of the page crossing the natural lines of the page. The same can be said about the text at the bottom of the pages before the footer. This text and the title could be placed in a way that would give more space and make the site look cleaner.
