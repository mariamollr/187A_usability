Visibility of system status: "The system should always keep users informed about what is going on, through appropriate feedback within reasonable time."







It is not easy to keep track of the system status at Louisiana.edu. The selected menu choice is not highlighted and the page title is not always consistent with the name of the selected menu choice. For example, the menu choice news has the page title 'communication and marketing' and the menu choice student life has the page title 'current students'. The users has to rely on their memory to know where they are and where they came from, which is inconvenient, especially for a site of this extensive size.

All the links on the site have the same red color that makes them easy to detect. When you hover over them, they are highlighted. This promotes system visibility by making the options clear. The fact that a global menu is present at all times makes it easy for the user to keep track of the main categories and the possibilities of where to go next.

The site does not have a site map. This is inconvenient on a site with so much information. The users simply have to click around to get an overview. This takes way to much time due to the poor information architecture and the extensive use of links, both to internal and external pages.
